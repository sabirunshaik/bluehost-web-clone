What are the Differences Between Shared, Dedicated and VPS Hosting?

Shared hosting is the most basic type of web hosting. It's a cost-effective option for small websites that don't require a lot of resources. With shared hosting, your website shares space on a server with other websites. This means that your website's performance may be affected by the performance of the other websites on the server.

Dedicated hosting is a more expensive option than shared hosting. With dedicated hosting, you have your own server that isn't shared with any other websites. This means that you have complete control over the server and its resources. Dedicated hosting is a good option for large websites or websites that require a lot of resources.

VPS hosting is a middle ground between shared hosting and dedicated hosting. With VPS hosting, you have your own virtual server that isn't shared with any other websites. However, you don't have complete control over the server as you would with dedicated hosting. VPS hosting is a good option for websites that need more resources than shared hosting can offer, but don't need the full resources of a dedicated server.
